# 16-Slide Blueprint: Standardizing on RHEL 9 (CentOS 7 Replacement)

## Slide 1 | Cover
RHEL 9 Enterprise Standardization — Proposal for [Public-Sector Organization]

## Slide 2 | Agenda
- Why act now
- Target platform and migration approach
- Security, patching, and support model
- Schedule, risk, and next steps

## Slide 3 | Why Act Now
- CentOS 7 has reached end of life — no further upstream security patches.
- Continued use increases exposure in an environment subject to public-sector oversight.
- ~200 servers are affected across the estate.

## Slide 4 | Recommended Target: RHEL 9
- Adopt RHEL 9 as the single enterprise Linux standard.
- Replaces CentOS 7 across all in-scope servers.
- Establishes one supported, standardized build going forward.

## Slide 5 | Migration Approach
- Phase 0: Inventory and dependency assessment for all 200 servers.
- Phase 1: Pilot migration on a small representative group.
- Phase 2: Wave-based migration of the remaining estate, sequenced by risk and criticality.

## Slide 6 | Lifecycle & Support Benefits
- Vendor-defined support lifecycle replaces the CentOS 7 community end-of-life situation.
- Structured patch and errata delivery instead of ad hoc community updates.
- Note: exact lifecycle end-dates and extended-support terms should be verified against current vendor documentation before the schedule is finalized.

## Slide 7 | Security & Compliance Approach
- Hardened baseline build (SELinux enforcing, restricted services, audit logging).
- The RFP references "security certification coverage" without naming the specific standard — this proposal treats the exact certification target as open and recommends confirming it with the customer early, since the hardening approach may need to be tailored accordingly.

## Slide 8 | Patch Management in a Disconnected Network
- Local, internally hosted repository mirror for all package and patch delivery.
- No direct outbound internet dependency for patching.
- Scheduled sync process to refresh the mirror on an approved cadence.

## Slide 9 | Operational Insight vs. Offline Requirement
- The RFP asks for both "fully offline operation" and "real-time cloud-based operational insights," which pull in different directions.
- Proposed approach: keep the environment offline for all standard operations, and treat any cloud-based insight capability as a separate, explicitly scoped exception requiring its own security review — not a default part of this migration.

## Slide 10 | Operational Automation
- Standard build process (automated OS install + baseline configuration) for all servers.
- Configuration drift checks against the approved baseline.
- Reduces reliance on manual, server-by-server administration.

## Slide 11 | Service Continuity
- The RFP requests "zero service interruption" but does not describe current high-availability capability for affected workloads.
- Where HA already exists, migration can be done with rolling cutover and minimal impact; where it does not, some workloads will require a scheduled maintenance window, which should be confirmed per application.

## Slide 12 | Comparison with Community Rebuild Distributions
- Community rebuilds depend on upstream re-publishing cycles and generally lack a single accountable support vendor.
- RHEL 9 provides a direct support relationship, which matters for a public-sector operating environment with formal support obligations.

## Slide 13 | Local Support & Escalation Model
- Tiered escalation: local operations → certified support partner → vendor backline.
- Support tier and response-time targets to be defined in the service agreement.

## Slide 14 | Migration Schedule
- Weeks 1-3: Assessment and pilot planning.
- Weeks 4-14: Wave-based production migration.
- Weeks 15-16: Stabilization and CentOS 7 decommission.

## Slide 15 | Risk & Mitigation
- Incompatible applications: identified during assessment, remediated or re-platformed before migration.
- Cutover risk: mitigated with pre-migration backups and a defined rollback path per wave.
- Open items (certification target, HA capability) tracked as pre-migration confirmation actions.

## Slide 16 | Next Steps
- Confirm the specific security certification requirement.
- Confirm HA status of in-scope workloads.
- Approve pilot group and schedule kickoff.
