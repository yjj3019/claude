# 16-Slide Proposal Blueprint: RHEL 9 Adoption for Public-Sector Linux Estate

## 1. Title
From CentOS 7 to RHEL 9 — A Standardized, Supported Linux Platform

## 2. Executive Summary
- CentOS 7 end-of-life leaves ~200 servers unpatched and unsupported.
- We propose migrating the estate to RHEL 9 as the single enterprise Linux standard.
- Approach balances continuity of operations with a modern, automatable platform.

## 3. Problem Statement
- CentOS 7 is past its end-of-life date; no further security patches are published upstream.
- Operating an EOL OS in a public-sector environment increases audit and compliance exposure.
- Server builds are currently inconsistent, complicating both patching and incident response.

## 4. Proposed Target Platform
- RHEL 9 as the standard OS image across all ~200 servers.
- Centralized subscription management and a single golden build.
- Consistent hardening baseline applied fleet-wide.

## 5. Migration Methodology
- Inventory and application dependency mapping for every server.
- Automated OS migration tooling (e.g., Leapp) where upgrade paths are supported.
- Reimage-and-restore path for hosts where in-place upgrade is not viable.

## 6. Lifecycle & Support Benefits
- RHEL 9 carries a defined multi-year support lifecycle with structured maintenance phases (exact end dates should be confirmed against the current Red Hat lifecycle page before contract signature) [unverified].
- Vendor-backed patch releases replace the current community-dependent model.
- Reduces risk of running unsupported software in production.

## 7. Security & Compliance
- The RFP requests "security certification coverage" without naming a specific standard; this proposal assumes alignment with common public-sector baselines (e.g., a CIS-style hardening benchmark) pending confirmation of the exact certification required.
- SELinux enforced by default; audit logging enabled fleet-wide.
- Recommend a follow-up session to pin down the exact certification scope before final sign-off.

## 8. Patch Management in a Disconnected Network
- Local, offline package mirror synchronized on a controlled schedule (e.g., via removable media or a one-way transfer gateway).
- All patch operations execute against the internal mirror — no direct internet dependency.

## 9. Operational Visibility Note
- The RFP also asks for "real-time cloud-based operational insights," which is in tension with the "fully offline operation" requirement.
- Recommended resolution: keep day-to-day operations fully offline, and use a periodic, one-way export of anonymized health/telemetry data to a cloud dashboard on a scheduled batch basis rather than a live connection — this preserves the offline posture while still giving near-real-time visibility. Final acceptance of this trade-off should be confirmed with the customer.

## 10. Automation & Standard Build
- Kickstart-based standard build for new servers.
- Configuration management enforces the golden image post-deployment.
- Drift reports generated on a recurring schedule.

## 11. Service Continuity
- The RFP requests "zero service interruption," but does not specify current workload HA capability; this plan assumes standard maintenance-window migration unless HA architecture is confirmed as already in place.
- Migration waves scheduled during low-traffic windows to minimize user impact.

## 12. Comparison with Community Rebuild Distributions
- Community rebuilds can lag official upstream releases and lack a single vendor accountable for support.
- RHEL 9 provides a direct vendor support path, which is significant in a public-sector operational context.

## 13. Local Support & Escalation Model
- Local Tier 1 operations team for day-to-day issues.
- Certified partner or vendor Tier 2/3 escalation for complex issues.
- Escalation paths and response-time targets to be defined in the support agreement.

## 14. Migration Schedule
- Weeks 1-4: Discovery, dependency mapping, pilot group selection.
- Weeks 5-16: Wave-based migration of remaining ~190 servers.
- Weeks 17-20: Validation, stabilization, and CentOS 7 decommission.

## 15. Risk & Mitigation
- Application incompatibility: mitigated via pre-migration testing in a staging environment.
- Data loss during cutover: mitigated via backups/snapshots before each migration wave.
- Certification/compliance scope uncertainty: mitigated by early confirmation workshop (see Slide 7).

## 16. Next Steps
- Confirm the specific security certification target.
- Confirm current HA/service-continuity capability of in-scope workloads.
- Approve pilot wave and schedule kickoff.
