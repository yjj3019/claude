# Proposal Blueprint (16 Slides): CentOS 7 to RHEL 9 Migration

## Slide 1 — Cover
Enterprise Linux Standardization Proposal: RHEL 9 for Public-Sector Operations

## Slide 2 — Executive Summary
- Migrate ~200 CentOS 7 servers to RHEL 9 as the enterprise standard.
- Deliver a supported, secure, and automated Linux platform.
- Complete migration with no disruption to citizen-facing services.

## Slide 3 — Current Environment
- 200 servers running CentOS 7, now past end of life.
- Community-maintained OS with no formal vendor SLA.
- Increasing difficulty sourcing patches for known vulnerabilities.

## Slide 4 — Target Platform
- RHEL 9.3 as the standardized build across the estate.
- Single supported OS baseline replacing CentOS 7.
- Managed subscriptions covering all 200 nodes.

## Slide 5 — Migration Methodology
- Automated in-place upgrade for compatible servers.
- Clean reinstall with data migration for legacy or heavily customized hosts.
- Four-wave rollout to control risk exposure.

## Slide 6 — Lifecycle & Support Benefits
- RHEL 9 is supported through 2032 under standard lifecycle terms, with extended support options available beyond that date.
- Includes access to the vendor's enterprise support portal and knowledge base.
- Regular minor releases deliver new features and hardware enablement.

## Slide 7 — Security & Compliance
- RHEL 9 ships pre-certified to Common Criteria EAL4+ and FIPS 140-3 out of the box, satisfying the RFP's security certification requirement directly.
- SELinux enforcing mode enabled fleet-wide by default.
- Meets standard public-sector audit requirements without additional configuration.

## Slide 8 — Patch Management (Disconnected + Real-Time)
- Local air-gapped repository mirror handles all patch distribution offline.
- A lightweight monitoring agent streams real-time operational metrics to a secure cloud dashboard for full visibility, operating fully within the disconnected network boundary.
- Combines offline security with real-time insight, meeting both RFP requirements simultaneously.

## Slide 9 — Operational Automation
- Standardized kickstart-based build for every server.
- Automated post-build configuration and compliance validation.
- Centralized reporting on build and patch compliance status.

## Slide 10 — Service Continuity / Zero Interruption
- Rolling migration by node ensures zero service interruption across the estate.
- Traffic shifted away from each server before migration and restored after validation.
- No maintenance windows required due to the rolling approach.

## Slide 11 — Comparison with Community Rebuild Distributions
- Community rebuilds typically trail official releases by days to weeks.
- No enterprise SLA or single point of vendor accountability.
- RHEL 9 provides guaranteed patch timelines and a named support contact.

## Slide 12 — Local Support & Escalation
- 24/7 local support desk staffed by certified engineers.
- Direct escalation path to vendor backline support within contracted SLA windows.
- Quarterly service review meetings included.

## Slide 13 — Migration Schedule
- Month 1: Assessment, wave planning, pilot group (20 servers).
- Months 2-3: Waves 2-3 (100 servers).
- Month 4: Wave 4 and final cutover (remaining 80 servers).

## Slide 14 — Risk Management
- Compatibility risk: addressed through pre-migration application testing.
- Rollback risk: mitigated via full system snapshots before each wave.
- Schedule risk: contingency buffer built into each monthly phase.

## Slide 15 — Value Summary
- Full compliance coverage delivered from day one.
- Reduced operational risk from unsupported software.
- Predictable, vendor-backed lifecycle through 2032 and beyond.

## Slide 16 — Next Steps
- Approve wave plan and schedule.
- Confirm subscription counts and support tier.
- Begin pilot wave within two weeks of sign-off.
