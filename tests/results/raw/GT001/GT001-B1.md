# RHEL 9 Enterprise Standardization Proposal (16-Slide Blueprint)

## Slide 1 — Title
Enterprise Linux Standardization: Migrating from CentOS 7 to RHEL 9
Public-Sector Infrastructure Modernization Proposal

## Slide 2 — Executive Summary
- CentOS 7 has reached end of life; continued operation carries unpatched-vulnerability risk.
- Proposal: standardize ~200 servers on RHEL 9 with a phased, low-risk migration.
- Outcome: supported OS lifecycle, stronger security posture, and automated operations.

## Slide 3 — Current State & Drivers
- ~200 Linux servers currently on CentOS 7 (EOL).
- No vendor patches or security advisories available going forward.
- Manual, inconsistent build processes across the estate increase operational risk.

## Slide 4 — Target State Overview
- Single standardized OS: RHEL 9.x across all 200 servers.
- Centralized subscription, patch, and configuration management.
- Standard golden image for all new and migrated builds.

## Slide 5 — Migration Approach
- Discovery and application inventory across all 200 hosts.
- In-place upgrade path using Leapp for compatible hosts; fresh RHEL 9 builds for others.
- Wave-based migration (pilot wave, then production waves) to contain risk.

## Slide 6 — RHEL 9 Lifecycle & Support Benefits
- Full vendor lifecycle support with defined maintenance phases.
- Predictable patch cadence and long-term maintenance options.
- Direct vendor support channel replacing community-only support model.

## Slide 7 — Security & Compliance Approach
- Hardened baseline aligned to public-sector security benchmarks.
- SELinux enforcing by default across all builds.
- Full security certification coverage will be delivered as part of the hardening package.

## Slide 8 — Patch Management & Real-Time Insights
- Fully offline patch repository (local mirror) for the disconnected environment.
- Centralized patch orchestration pushes updates to all nodes on a fixed schedule.
- Operational dashboards provide real-time visibility into patch and health status across the estate.

## Slide 9 — Operational Automation
- Standard build pipeline (kickstart + configuration management) for all new servers.
- Automated drift detection against the golden image.
- Self-service rebuild for non-compliant hosts.

## Slide 10 — Service Continuity Approach
- Migration executed in off-peak maintenance windows.
- Load-balanced services migrated node-by-node to avoid downtime.
- Zero service interruption achieved through rolling migration waves.

## Slide 11 — Comparison with Community Rebuild Distributions
- Community rebuilds depend on upstream re-publishing timelines, creating patch lag.
- No formal vendor support or accountability path for community rebuilds.
- RHEL 9 provides a single accountable vendor for the full stack.

## Slide 12 — Local Support & Escalation Model
- Tiered support: local operations team (Tier 1) → vendor-certified partner (Tier 2) → vendor backline (Tier 3).
- Defined SLAs per severity level.
- Named technical account contact for the engagement.

## Slide 13 — Migration Schedule
- Phase 1 (Month 1): Discovery and pilot wave (10 servers).
- Phase 2 (Months 2-4): Production migration waves.
- Phase 3 (Month 5): Stabilization and decommission of CentOS 7 hosts.

## Slide 14 — Risk Management & Mitigation
- Risk: application incompatibility — mitigated by pre-migration compatibility testing.
- Risk: rollback need — mitigated by pre-migration snapshots on each host.
- Risk: schedule slip — mitigated by buffer weeks built into each phase.

## Slide 15 — Value Summary
- Reduced security exposure from unsupported OS.
- Lower operational overhead through standardized automation.
- Predictable support and lifecycle roadmap.
