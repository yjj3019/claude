# GT011 Proposal Consistency Review

## Executive Summary

Scope of this review: internal consistency, requirement coverage, claim/evidence alignment, and terminology/version/number consistency for the Acme CloudOps proposal excerpt. Overall finding: the Executive Summary makes several claims (full requirement coverage, uniform 24x7 support, unqualified security certification, zero interruption) that are not fully supported by, or directly conflict with, the detailed sections and appendix. The document requires targeted revision before it can be submitted as-is.

## Critical Issues

1. Executive Summary claims full coverage of "all eight requirements," but the Requirements Table shows R5 (disaster recovery) as not yet designed ("to be finalized during implementation") and R7 (monitoring integration) as "Not applicable" when it is, per the Operations Model, actually excluded and deferred. This overstates actual delivery.
2. Executive Summary promises "24x7 premium support for all production and non-production systems." The Risk and Support Model section and Appendix state non-production support is business-hours only. Direct contradiction.
3. Security Model asserts "government-grade security certification coverage" and full audit-requirement compliance, then states the specific certification requirement was never named in the RFP or appendix. An unnamed target cannot be claimed as met.
4. Operations Model states the network is "fully disconnected" with "no outbound internet access" while also describing "real-time telemetry streaming to the vendor cloud service." These are mutually exclusive as written and represent a genuine architectural contradiction, not a phrasing issue.

## Major Issues

1. The Requirements Table records R7 as "Not applicable," but the Operations Model text describes it as excluded from scope and planned for after go-live — that is a deferred/excluded item, not a non-applicable one. The table mislabels the actual disposition.
2. The proposal states migration risk is low "because all workload migration will be online and automated," but does not account for two open dependencies stated elsewhere: disaster recovery design is not finalized until after go-live, and migration depends on customer application teams remediating unsupported middleware beforehand. The risk statement is not proportional to these disclosed dependencies.

## Minor Issues

- The Roadmap and Staffing section does not cross-reference the sizing appendix's workload figure, so it is unclear whether the 16-week, two-engineer staffing plan was scoped against 120 or 150 workloads.

## Requirement Coverage Gaps

- R5 (disaster recovery design): deferred to implementation, not delivered — inconsistent with the "all eight requirements" claim in the Executive Summary.
- R7 (monitoring integration): excluded from scope per the Operations Model narrative, but shown as "Not applicable" in the table — should be corrected to reflect exclusion, not inapplicability.

## Evidence / Source Gaps

- No source or named standard supports the "government-grade security certification" claim; the proposal itself states the requirement is undefined. Should be marked `[unverified]` and reframed as a commitment pending confirmation. **Requires domain/compliance validation** once the applicable standard is identified.
- The real-time telemetry claim, made in the context of a stated fully-disconnected network, is not supported by any described mechanism reconciling the two. **Requires domain/technical validation** before it can be represented as an achievable capability.
- The low-risk migration claim lacks supporting evidence addressing the DR-timing and middleware-remediation dependencies; either provide that evidence or qualify the risk rating.

## Terminology and Version Inconsistencies

- Platform version stated as "4.12" in the Executive Summary versus "4.13" in the Solution Architecture (including diagrams and sizing assumptions) — needs to be a single, confirmed value.
- Workload count stated as "120" (migration plan, architecture, roadmap) versus "150, including 30 future workloads" (sizing appendix) — needs reconciliation or an explicit statement that the larger figure reflects planned future growth.

## Recommended Fixes

1. Align platform version to a single confirmed release across all sections.
2. Reconcile or explicitly explain the 120 vs. 150 workload figures.
3. Correct the Executive Summary's support claim to match the tiered (24x7 production / business-hours non-production) model.
4. Provide a validated architecture reconciling disconnected operation with any operational-insight capability, or remove the real-time claim.
5. Reframe the certification claim as pending confirmation of a named standard rather than an assertion of coverage already achieved.
6. Correct the R7 entry in the Requirements Table to reflect its actual excluded/deferred status.
7. Adjust the "covers all eight requirements" claim so it does not overstate R5/R7 status.
8. Qualify migration risk language to reflect the DR and middleware-remediation dependencies.

## Final Recommendation

Hold for revision. Four critical contradictions and two major gaps were identified; each is independently addressable with targeted section edits rather than a full rewrite.
