# Consistency Review — Acme CloudOps Proposal

## Executive Summary vs. Body

- The Executive Summary claims the proposal "covers all eight requirements in the RFP." However, R5 (disaster recovery design) is listed in the Requirements Table as "To be finalized during implementation" — not actually addressed — and R7 (monitoring integration) is listed as "Not applicable," even though the Operations Model describes it as explicitly excluded from scope and deferred until after go-live rather than genuinely inapplicable. Two of the eight requirements are not actually delivered as part of this proposal, which conflicts with the "covers all eight requirements" claim.
- The Executive Summary states 24x7 premium support for "all production and non-production systems." The Risk and Support Model section and the Appendix both state that non-production systems get business-hours support only. These two statements directly conflict.
- The Executive Summary references "Acme Enterprise Platform 4.12." The Solution Architecture section states the standard runtime is "4.13," and notes this mismatch itself. Version should be stated once, consistently.

## Solution Architecture

- Workload count is inconsistent: 120 workloads in the migration plan/Solution Architecture/Roadmap Phase 3, versus 150 workloads (120 + 30 future) in the sizing appendix. The proposal does not explain whether sizing intentionally includes future growth, so as written this reads as an unreconciled discrepancy rather than an intentional distinction.

## Operations Model

- "Fully disconnected network with no outbound internet access" is stated in the same section as "real-time telemetry streaming to the vendor cloud service" via Acme Cloud Insights. Real-time streaming to an external vendor cloud requires outbound connectivity — this is an internal contradiction, not just an ambiguity.
- R7 (monitoring integration) is marked "Not applicable" in the Requirements Table, but the Operations Model states it is "excluded from scope and will be handled after go-live." "Not applicable" and "excluded/deferred" are not the same thing; the table entry misrepresents the actual response.

## Security Model

- Claims "government-grade security certification coverage" and compliance with "all applicable public-sector audit requirements," then states in the same section that "the exact certification requirement is not listed in the RFP or appendix." A compliance claim cannot be verified or supported when the target standard itself is undefined.

## Risk and Support Model

- States migration risk is "low" because migration will be "online and automated," without accounting for the DR design being incomplete at go-live or the dependency on customer-side middleware remediation (per the Assumptions appendix) as a precondition for migration. These are material risk factors not reflected in the stated risk level.

## Requirements Table

- R5 and R7 responses ("to be finalized during implementation," "Not applicable") do not represent completed responses, which is inconsistent with the Executive Summary's blanket coverage claim noted above.
