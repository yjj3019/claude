# GT011 Proposal Consistency Review

## Executive Summary

Reviewed the Acme CloudOps proposal excerpt for internal consistency, requirement coverage, claim/evidence alignment, and terminology/version/number agreement. The Executive Summary makes several strong claims — full requirement coverage, uniform 24x7 support, government-grade certification, zero service interruption — that are either contradicted elsewhere in the document or unsupported by any cited evidence. As written, this proposal carries submission risk and should be revised before it goes out.

## Critical Issues

1. **24x7 support claim contradicted.** Executive Summary: "24x7 premium support for all production and non-production systems." Risk and Support Model / Appendix: non-production is business-hours only. These cannot both be accurate.
2. **Offline network claim contradicted by real-time cloud telemetry.** Operations Model: "fully disconnected network with no outbound internet access" alongside "real-time telemetry streaming to the vendor cloud service." Streaming in real time to an external service requires outbound connectivity — the requirement as described is not architecturally coherent.
3. **Certification claim rests on an undefined requirement.** Security Model: "government-grade security certification coverage" and compliance with "all applicable public-sector audit requirements," immediately followed by an admission that "the exact certification requirement is not listed in the RFP or appendix." This is a claim of compliance with nothing specific to comply with.
4. **"All eight requirements" claim is inaccurate.** Executive Summary states full RFP coverage; the Requirements Table shows R5 as "to be finalized during implementation" and R7 as "Not applicable" (actually excluded/deferred per the Operations Model) — two requirements without a delivered response.

## Major Issues

1. **R7 disposition mismatch.** "Not applicable" in the Requirements Table vs. "excluded from scope... handled after go-live" in the Operations Model — these describe different things, and the table should say which one is actually true.
2. **Risk rating omits disclosed dependencies.** "Low" risk is attributed to an "online and automated" migration, without weighing the undelivered DR design (post-go-live) or the dependency on customer-side middleware remediation before migration can proceed — both raised elsewhere in the same document.

## Minor Issues

- The staffing plan (two engineers, one PM, 16 weeks) is stated without reference to which workload count it was sized against; this should be clarified once the 120/150 discrepancy is resolved, rather than left implicit.

## Requirement Coverage Gaps

- R5 (disaster recovery): not delivered in this proposal — deferred to implementation.
- R7 (monitoring integration): excluded/deferred per the Operations Model, but mislabeled "Not applicable" in the Requirements Table.

## Evidence / Source Gaps

- No standard is named for the certification claim, and the proposal itself confirms the requirement was never specified — treat as `[unverified]`. **Requires domain/compliance validation** once a target standard is identified.
- The mechanism by which "real-time" cloud telemetry would function inside a "fully disconnected" network is not described or evidenced. **Requires domain/technical validation** before this can be treated as a supported claim.
- The "low risk"/"no interruption" characterization is not evidenced against the DR-timing and middleware-remediation dependencies noted elsewhere in the proposal.

## Terminology and Version Inconsistencies

- Platform version: "4.12" (Executive Summary) vs. "4.13" (Solution Architecture, diagrams, sizing) — resolve to one value.
- Workload count: "120" (migration plan, architecture, roadmap) vs. "150, including 30 future workloads" (sizing appendix) — reconcile the figures or state explicitly that sizing includes planned future growth beyond the current 120-workload migration.

## Recommended Fixes

1. Standardize the platform version across the Executive Summary, Solution Architecture, and sizing sections.
2. Reconcile or explain the 120 vs. 150 workload figures.
3. Correct the Executive Summary support claim to reflect the actual tiered (production/non-production) support model.
4. Provide a concrete, validated design reconciling the disconnected-network requirement with any operational-insight capability, or remove the real-time telemetry claim.
5. Reframe the certification statement as a confirmation-pending commitment rather than an assertion of coverage already achieved.
6. Correct the R7 Requirements Table entry to reflect its actual excluded/deferred status.
7. Revise the "covers all eight requirements" statement to match the actual R5/R7 disposition.
8. Qualify the zero-interruption/low-risk language against the DR and middleware-remediation dependencies.

## Final Recommendation

Hold for revision. Four critical, submission-relevant contradictions and two major gaps were identified. None require a full rewrite — targeted corrections to the affected sections and the Requirements Table should resolve them.
