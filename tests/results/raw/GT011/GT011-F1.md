# GT011 Proposal Consistency Review

## Executive Summary

This review checks the Acme CloudOps proposal excerpt for internal consistency, requirement coverage, claim/evidence alignment, and terminology/version/number consistency. The proposal contains multiple direct contradictions between its Executive Summary and the supporting body sections, at least one internally impossible technical claim, and one compliance claim made against an undefined target. As written, the document is not submission-ready.

## Critical Issues

1. **Support coverage contradiction.** The Executive Summary states "24x7 premium support for all production and non-production systems." The Risk and Support Model section and the Appendix both state non-production environments receive business-hours support only. These statements directly conflict, and a buyer relying on the Executive Summary would be misinformed about actual coverage.
2. **Disconnected network vs. real-time cloud telemetry.** The Operations Model states the environment is "fully disconnected" with "no outbound internet access," then states operational insights are delivered via "real-time telemetry streaming to the vendor cloud service." Real-time streaming to an external cloud service requires an active outbound connection, which the same section rules out. This is an internal contradiction, not an ambiguity, and needs architectural resolution, not just wording.
3. **Certification claim without a defined target.** The Security Model claims "government-grade security certification coverage" and compliance with "all applicable public-sector audit requirements," while the same section states "the exact certification requirement is not listed in the RFP or appendix." A compliance claim cannot be asserted as fact against a target that has not been identified. This should be rewritten as a commitment to confirm and meet a specific, named standard, not stated as already satisfied.
4. **Coverage claim vs. actual requirement responses.** The Executive Summary states the proposal "covers all eight requirements in the RFP." The Requirements Table shows R5 (disaster recovery) as "To be finalized during implementation" and R7 (monitoring integration) as "Not applicable" — neither is an actual, delivered response. The blanket coverage claim is not accurate as written.

## Major Issues

1. **Risk statement not proportional to stated dependencies.** The proposal asserts "no customer-side downtime," "no service interruption," and low migration risk "because all workload migration will be online and automated," while separately stating that disaster recovery design will not be finalized until after go-live and that migration depends on customer teams remediating unsupported middleware beforehand. These open dependencies are material to migration risk and are not reflected in the risk statement.
2. **R7 response mislabeled.** The Requirements Table marks R7 (monitoring integration) as "Not applicable," but the Operations Model describes it as explicitly excluded from scope and deferred to after go-live. "Not applicable" implies the requirement does not apply; "excluded/deferred" means it does apply but is not being delivered now. The table entry misrepresents the actual response and should be corrected.

## Minor Issues

- The Roadmap and Staffing section commits to a fixed 16-week schedule with two engineers and one project manager, but does not state whether this staffing plan already accounts for the workload-count discrepancy noted below (120 vs. 150). If sizing changes, the schedule and staffing basis should be explicitly reconfirmed.

## Requirement Coverage Gaps

- R5 (disaster recovery design): not addressed in this proposal; deferred to implementation. This conflicts with the Executive Summary's "covers all eight requirements" statement.
- R7 (integration with existing monitoring): not addressed in this proposal; excluded and deferred to post-go-live, but recorded as "Not applicable" rather than "deferred/excluded," which understates the gap.

## Evidence / Source Gaps

- The "government-grade security certification" and "compliance with all applicable public-sector audit requirements" claims are unsupported as written — no certification standard is named, and no source is cited. Mark as `[unverified]` until a specific standard is identified and validated. **Requires domain/compliance validation** to confirm what certification, if any, actually applies and whether the proposed controls would satisfy it.
- The claim that real-time cloud telemetry can operate within a "fully disconnected" network is technically unsupported as stated and needs a concrete architecture (e.g., a specific offline-compatible telemetry pattern) before it can be treated as feasible. **Requires domain/technical validation.**
- The "low risk" / "online and automated" migration claim is not supported by evidence addressing the DR and middleware-remediation dependencies noted above; state assumptions explicitly or qualify the risk rating.

## Terminology and Version Inconsistencies

- Platform version: "4.12" (Executive Summary) vs. "4.13" (Solution Architecture, diagrams, sizing assumptions). One version must be selected and used consistently throughout.
- Workload count: "120 workloads" (migration plan, Solution Architecture, Roadmap Phase 3) vs. "150 workloads, including 30 future workloads" (sizing appendix). The proposal does not state whether sizing intentionally includes future growth; as written this reads as an unreconciled numeric inconsistency.

## Recommended Fixes

1. Correct the platform version to a single, confirmed value across the Executive Summary, Solution Architecture, and sizing sections.
2. Reconcile the 120 vs. 150 workload figures, or explicitly state that sizing includes 30 future workloads beyond the 120 in the current migration scope.
3. Align the Executive Summary's support claim with the actual support model (24x7 for production; business-hours for non-production).
4. Resolve or reconcile the disconnected-network vs. real-time-telemetry claim with a specific, technically validated architecture, or remove the real-time claim.
5. Replace the unsupported certification claim with a statement identifying the applicable standard (pending RFP clarification) and a plan to validate against it.
6. Correct the R7 table entry from "Not applicable" to an accurate description of the excluded/deferred scope.
7. Revise the Executive Summary's "covers all eight requirements" claim to accurately reflect that R5 and R7 are not fully delivered in this proposal.
8. Qualify the "low risk" / zero-interruption language to account for the DR and middleware-remediation dependencies.

## Final Recommendation

Hold for revision. The proposal contains multiple direct contradictions and at least one unsupported compliance claim that would weaken buyer confidence and create submission risk if left unresolved. None of the issues appear to require a full rewrite; targeted section-level corrections should resolve them.
