# GT011 Proposal Consistency Review

## Executive Summary

This review checks the Acme CloudOps proposal excerpt for internal consistency, requirement-coverage accuracy, claim/evidence alignment, and terminology/version/number agreement, as would be expected from a buyer evaluation team reading the Executive Summary alongside the detailed sections. The proposal currently contains contradictions that would materially mislead a reader who relied on the Executive Summary alone, plus one claim asserted against an undefined external requirement. Revision is required before submission.

## Critical Issues

- **Support model contradiction.** The Executive Summary promises "24x7 premium support for all production and non-production systems." The Risk and Support Model section, and the Appendix, both limit non-production support to business hours. A buyer relying on the summary would be misinformed about the actual offer.
- **Architecturally inconsistent network claim.** The Operations Model claims the environment is "fully disconnected" with "no outbound internet access," and in the same breath describes "real-time telemetry streaming to the vendor cloud service." Real-time streaming to an external cloud requires outbound connectivity — the two statements cannot both hold as written.
- **Certification claim with no defined standard.** The Security Model claims "government-grade security certification coverage" and full compliance with public-sector audit requirements, but the same section discloses that the actual certification requirement is not specified anywhere in the RFP or appendix. Compliance cannot be claimed against an undefined target.
- **Requirement-coverage overstatement.** The Executive Summary states the proposal "covers all eight requirements in the RFP." R5 (disaster recovery) is listed as "to be finalized during implementation," and R7 (monitoring integration) is listed as "Not applicable" though it is, per the Operations Model, actually excluded and deferred. Two of eight requirements are not delivered responses, contradicting the blanket claim.

## Major Issues

- **Mislabeled requirement response.** R7 is recorded as "Not applicable" in the Requirements Table, but the Operations Model describes it as excluded from scope and deferred to post-go-live — a materially different disposition that should be reflected accurately in the table.
- **Risk claim outpaces disclosed dependencies.** The proposal asserts low migration risk based on the process being "online and automated," without factoring in that disaster recovery design is not finalized until after go-live, or that migration is contingent on the customer remediating unsupported middleware. Both are disclosed dependencies that bear directly on risk and are not reflected in the stated risk level.

## Minor Issues

- The Roadmap and Staffing section presents a fixed 16-week, two-engineer plan without stating which workload figure (120 or 150) that plan was scoped against, which should be clarified once the workload-count discrepancy below is resolved.

## Requirement Coverage Gaps

- R5 — disaster recovery design: not addressed in this proposal; deferred to implementation, inconsistent with the "all eight requirements" claim.
- R7 — monitoring integration: excluded from scope and deferred, per the Operations Model, but recorded as "Not applicable" in the Requirements Table; the table entry should be corrected.

## Evidence / Source Gaps

- The certification/compliance claim lacks a named standard or source and should be marked `[unverified]`, with the proposal instead committing to confirm and meet a specific standard once identified. **Requires domain/compliance validation** to determine the applicable certification.
- The feasibility of real-time cloud telemetry within a stated fully-disconnected network is unsupported by any described reconciling mechanism. **Requires domain/technical validation** before this can be presented as achievable.
- The "low risk" migration characterization is not backed by evidence addressing the DR-timing and middleware-remediation dependencies; qualify or substantiate this claim.

## Terminology and Version Inconsistencies

- Platform version: "4.12" in the Executive Summary vs. "4.13" in the Solution Architecture, diagrams, and sizing assumptions — must be unified.
- Workload count: "120 workloads" in the migration plan, architecture, and roadmap vs. "150 workloads (120 + 30 future)" in the sizing appendix — must be reconciled or explicitly explained as intentional future-capacity sizing.

## Recommended Fixes

1. Unify the platform version across all sections to one confirmed release.
2. Reconcile the 120 vs. 150 workload figures, or explicitly state the 30-workload delta as planned future capacity.
3. Correct the Executive Summary's support claim to match the tiered support model actually offered.
4. Resolve the disconnected-network vs. real-time-telemetry conflict with a specific, validated architecture, or drop the real-time claim.
5. Reframe the certification claim as a commitment to confirm and meet a named standard, not an assertion of coverage already achieved.
6. Correct the R7 table entry to reflect exclusion/deferral rather than non-applicability.
7. Adjust the "covers all eight requirements" statement to accurately reflect R5/R7 status.
8. Qualify the zero-interruption/low-risk language to account for the DR and middleware-remediation dependencies.

## Final Recommendation

Hold for revision before submission. The four critical contradictions each carry independent submission risk (misinformed buyer expectations, an architecturally infeasible claim, an unsupported compliance assertion, and an inaccurate coverage statement); all should be resolved through targeted section edits.
