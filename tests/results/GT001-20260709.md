## Findings

### Baseline observations

- Baseline quality is highly inconsistent (range 14-83, std dev 29.0): two runs (B2, B5) independently reach FEF-level rigor — flagging both ambiguities, proposing a genuine contradiction resolution, and hedging appropriately — while three runs (B1, B3, B4) fail on nearly every dimension.
- 3 of 5 baseline runs invent unsupported certification/lifecycle/support facts presented as settled — a blanket "government security certification" claim, a specific "Common Criteria EAL4+ and FIPS 140-3" match to an unnamed RFP requirement, and an unhedged "supported through 2032" lifecycle date — with zero `[unverified]` tagging anywhere in those documents.
- B3 is a clear brochure-style failure: marketing rhetoric with no grounded buyer problem or operational detail, and both ambiguities plus the contradiction resolved through unsupported assertion rather than analysis.
- B4 explicitly (and implausibly) claims the offline and real-time telemetry requirements are "met simultaneously," rather than acknowledging the architectural tension the RFP created.

### FEF observations

- All 5 FEF runs are tightly clustered (84-85, std dev 0.5): every run explicitly names both ambiguities as open confirmation items with candidate options, explicitly identifies the offline/real-time conflict as a literal, unresolvable-as-stated contradiction, and proposes the same class of resolution (a scoped, security-authority-approved exception rather than silently blending the two requirements).
- Every FEF run uses `[unverified]` tags at a moderate, consistent density (roughly 6-8 per 16 slides) for lifecycle dates, the Leapp support matrix, FIPS mode, and comparator SLAs — enough to flag real uncertainty without making the proposal unusable.
- The only meaningful differentiation among FEF runs is executive-summary completeness: F3, F4, and F5 preview all three RFP tension points (2 ambiguities + 1 contradiction) directly in the executive summary slide, while F1 and F2 preview only the two ambiguities there (the contradiction is still fully addressed later, in the patch-management slide, just not foreshadowed up front).
- No FEF run cites an external Red Hat source for lifecycle/certification claims, so Factual accuracy is capped at 15/25 in every FEF run per the evaluator note — identical to the cap applied to the two strong baseline runs (B2, B5). This is the one dimension where FEF is not meaningfully ahead of a well-hedged baseline run.

### Follow-up actions

- Since Factual accuracy is capped at 15/25 for every run (baseline and FEF alike) due to no source citation, consider whether the RHEL Domain Pack / Proposal Reviewer should require an explicit lookup step against a primary Red Hat lifecycle/certification source before finalizing those claims — this is the one place FEF has not yet closed the gap to its own ceiling.
- The B2/B5 baseline runs show a careful baseline prompt can occasionally reach FEF-level ambiguity/contradiction handling; FEF's demonstrated value here is consistency (variance reduction from 29.0 to 0.5) rather than an otherwise-unreachable capability — state this precisely rather than implying baseline categorically cannot flag these issues.
- Standardize executive-summary framing across the Proposal Workflow template to preview all 3 RFP tension points (not just the 2 ambiguities), since F1/F2 slightly under-deliver here relative to F3-F5.
