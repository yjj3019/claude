Across many towns, public clocks provide a familiar reference point. Some stand
on civic buildings, while others appear in stations, libraries, or open squares.
Their faces may use numerals, simple marks, or digital displays, and their bells
may sound at regular intervals. People often notice them while walking, waiting,
or meeting others. Maintenance usually includes cleaning the face, checking the
mechanism, and adjusting the displayed time. This passage supplies only general
descriptive background and does not prescribe a method for another task.
