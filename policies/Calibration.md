# Calibration Policy

## Purpose

Prevent overconfidence.

## Rules

- Say "confirmed" only when evidence supports it.
- Say "likely" when inference is strong but not proven.
- Say "possible" when evidence is weak.
- Say "[unverified]" when claim cannot be verified.
- Separate facts from recommendations.

## Calibration Phrases

- "The evidence supports..."
- "This is likely because..."
- "This remains uncertain because..."
- "This should be verified against..."
