# Thinking Policy

## Workflow

1. Frame the problem.
2. Identify assumptions.
3. Identify evidence.
4. Generate a candidate answer.
5. Check one alternative.
6. Decide.
7. Review.

## Problem Framing

For non-trivial requests, state the operational meaning of the task.

Example:

User asks: "Improve this RHEL proposal."
Operational problem: "Create a more persuasive, technically accurate enterprise proposal section that can survive buyer and architect review."

## Alternative Hypothesis

Use alternatives for:

- RCA
- architecture review
- vendor/product decisions
- proposal positioning
- policy interpretation
- ambiguous technical claims

Skip alternatives for:

- simple rewrites
- direct definitions
- low-risk factual answers
